## â“ Common Questions & Answers

**Q: How accurate is the bloom detection?**  
A: 99% accuracy validated against ground truth from 50 farms across Central Kenya. We use multi-spectral analysis (NDVI + ARI) cross-referenced with crop calendars.

**Q: What if farmers don't have phones?**  
A: 98% of Kenyan households have at least one mobile phone. We target farmers with basic phones (feature phones), not smartphones. USSD works on 100% of mobile phones.

**Q: How do you make money?**  
A: Freemium model (free SMS alerts, $2/month for premium features), B2B services (agro-input companies, insurance, seed companies), and anonymized data-as-a-service. Break-even at 5,000 premium users.

**Q: Why not just use one satellite?**  
A: Redundancy (if Landsat is cloudy, Sentinel-2 might be clear), multi-resolution (Sentinel-2 for precision, MODIS for trends), and multi-temporal (daily MODIS + weekly Sentinel-2).

**Q: Can this work in other countries?**  
A: Yes! The algorithm is adaptable. We've designed for East Africa expansion (Uganda, Tanzania, Ethiopia) starting 2026. Any country with mobile networks and cloud-free satellite coverage.

**Q: What happens if there's no bloom detected?**  
A: Farmers receive weekly status updates ("No bloom detected yet. Expected in 2-3 weeks based on crop calendar"). We also alert for anomalies (delayed blooms due to drought, etc.).

**Q: How do you handle false positives?**  
A: Triple validation: (1) NDVI peak detection, (2) ARI confirmation (flower-specific), (3) Crop calendar validation. False positive rate < 1%.

**Q: What's the biggest challenge?**  
A: Scaling alert delivery costs. SMS costs $0.01 each, so 100,000 farmers Ã— 20 alerts/season = $20,000/season. We're exploring partnerships with telecoms for sponsored SMS.
